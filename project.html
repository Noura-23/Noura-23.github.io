Project Overview

This project challenges conventional narratives about electricity access in Sub-Saharan Africa by examining how structural choices, institutional capacity, and resource allocation strategies create diverging energy trajectories across the continent. Rather than treating SSA as a monolithic region, this analysis reveals three distinct "Energy Africas" emerging from fundamentally different approaches to power system development.

The analysis moves beyond simple access metrics to explore the hidden dimensions of energy systems, including the parallel diesel-powered economy, the relationship between institutional capacity and investment flows, and the critical question of whether renewable energy is actually closing the electricity gap or simply creating new forms of energy inequality.
